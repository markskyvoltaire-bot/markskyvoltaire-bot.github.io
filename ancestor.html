Luce Voltaire (c. 1802)

Born in Trois-Rivières, Guadeloupe, Luce Voltaire emerged into a world of hardship. Brave, cunning, and nurturing, she survived and guided her descendants across centuries. A voodoo priestess and seer, she could communicate with spirits, manipulate elements symbolically, and whisper guidance to chosen bloodlines. She embodies freedom, memory, justice, and resilience—always watching over her lineage.

Her presence is signaled by a faint shimmer, the beat of a hummingbird's wings, and whispers carried on the wind. Garbed in earth-toned robes threaded with deep red and blue, and carrying talismans of beads and carved wood, she stands as a living link to the ancestors.
